Explore the Planets

Here’s a breakdown of the planets in our solar system, their distance from the Sun, and other interesting facts:

Planet Distance from the Sun (Million km) Diameter (km) Interesting Fact
Mercury 57.9 4,880 Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system.
Venus 108.2 12,104 Venus has the hottest surface of any planet, with temperatures of 460°C.
Earth 149.6 12,742 Earth is the only planet known to support life.
Mars 227.9 6,779 Mars is home to the tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.
Jupiter 778.5 139,820 Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system.
Saturn 1,434 116,460 Saturn is famous for its prominent ring system.
Uranus 2,871 50,724 Uranus rotates on its side, making its axis almost parallel to the Sun.
Neptune 4,495 49,244 Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching 2,100 km/h.
